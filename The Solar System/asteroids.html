Asteroids

Close

Asteroids come in billions, in all shapes and sizes. Some are tiny pebbles, some are hundreds of kilometers in size. Some are perfectly round, some are very strange shapes. Some travel alone, some go in pairs, and some have tiny moons orbiting them. Together… they don’t add up too much. Combined they would have only 4% the mass of our Moon. Asteroids smaller than 1 meters are usually called meteoroids, although there is no clear distinction between the two classes.

The asteroid belt

The largest asteroid in the Solar System by quite a margin, considered a dwarf planet just like plutoids, sizing 950km across. It is the first ever asteroid to be discovered, and at the time of discovery in 1801 it was thought to be a planet. Only after multiple more objects were found orbiting in the same area, it was stripped off its status as a planet in the 1850s. Recent findings (2016) suggest that there might be geological activity present in Ceres, with salts appearing in its surface in many different spots.

Meteors

multiple small meteors burn up in the atmosphere, giving the effect of a shooting star. It is estimated that about 15 million kg of meteors fall on Earth every year. Few meteors are big enough to survive the burn through the atmosphere, and collide with Earth’s surface. Because of their speed of multiple kilometers per second, they can cause devastating damage. Besides asteroids, also comets can become meteors.
